Privacy Policy

Effective Date: February 08, 2017

Doodlio LLC ("us", "we", or "our") operates the https://www.doodlio.com website and the Doodlio mobile application (the "Service").

Doodlio provides a business service to churches and schools (our "Clients"). As part of the delivery of that service, our Clients may provide us with confidential information, including personally identifiable information about administrators, faculty, staff, members of the church or school community, parents, and students. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use and disclose confidential information, including personally identifiable information.

General Policies
We collect personal information for the purpose of providing the Service to our Clients. The information is owned by our Clients and we do not rent, sell, lease, or otherwise share personal information with anyone except as noted in this Privacy Policy.

We use your Personal Information for providing and improving the Service. By using the Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

We will disclose your Personal Information where required to do so by law or subpoena or if we believe that such action is necessary to comply with the law and the reasonable requests of law enforcement, to enforce our Terms of Service, or to protect the security or integrity of our Service.

If you are a member of a church or school that is one of our Clients and would like to view the information that is collected about you or a family member, or to request removal of your information from our Service, please contact your church or school directly.

We may employ third party companies and individuals to facilitate our Service, to provide the Service on behalf of our Clients, to perform Service-related services and/or to assist us and our Clients in analyzing how our Service is used. These third parties have access to your Personal Information only to perform specific tasks on our behalf and on behalf of our Clients and are obligated not to disclose or use your information for any other purpose.

How is Children's Personal Information Treated?
While using our Service, our Clients generally collect and maintain information about children that includes, but is not limited to:

Churches

Demographic information including full name, address, date of birth, and family relationships Information about participation in church ministries, attendance at events, and sacraments Schools

Demographic information including full name, address, date of birth, and family relationships Academic information such as classes taken, assignments, grades, and attendance
Participation in school activities such as sports teams, band, choir, and clubs
We collect this information for the purpose of providing the Service to our Clients. The information is owned by our Clients and we do not rent, sell, lease, or otherwise share children's information with anyone, except in keeping with the General Policies defined above.

Some churches or schools may provide children with online access to the Doodlio website or mobile apps in order to allow the child to participate in certain activities, including but limited to:

Review class assignments or submit completed work
View messages and calendar events, or participate in group discussions for classrooms, church ministries, and sports teams
Sign up for activities such as church ministry events
Doodlio does not allow anyone under the age of 13 to access our Service without prior approval from a parent or guardian. Per our Terms of Service, our Clients have agreed to obtain parental consent prior to issuing access codes that will allow children to use our system. In addition to parental consent requirements, Doodlio provides additional safeguards designed to protect children who use our system, including:

Automatic links between the child's account and parent accounts so that parents can view all of their child's activities, messages, files, events, and other information
All Direct Messages (one-to-one messaging) sent or received by a child are automatically copied to parents
School and church administrators have the ability restrict or disable the specific types of access available to children, including messaging and file sharing
The ability for church and school administrators to view and search all information in their community
If you are a member of a Doodlio Client community whose child has access to the Service and you did not give parental consent, please contact your church or school community, or you may contact us at privacy@doodlio.com.

Automatically Collected Information
We may also collect information that your browser sends whenever you visit our Service or when you access the Service by or through a mobile device.

This data may include information such as your computer's Internet Protocol ("IP") address, browser type, browser version, the pages of our Service that you visit, the time and date of your visit, the time spent on those pages and other statistics.

When you access the Service by or through a mobile device, this data may include information such as the type of mobile device you use, your mobile device unique ID, the IP address of your mobile device, your mobile operating system, the type of mobile Internet browser you use and other statistics.

In addition, we may use third party services such as Google Analytics that collect, monitor and analyze this type of information in order to increase our Service's functionality. These third party service providers have their own privacy policies addressing how they use such information.

Cookies are files with a small amount of data, which may include an anonymous unique identifier. Cookies are sent to your browser from a web site and transferred to your device. We use cookies to collect information in order to improve our services for you.

You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. The Help feature on most browsers provide information on how to accept cookies, disable cookies or to notify you when receiving a new cookie.

If you do not accept cookies, you may not be able to use some features of our Service and we recommend that you leave them turned on.

We may from time to time share aggregated data to our Clients and partners so that they may better understand how you use our system. The aggregated data we share does not include any personally identifiable information.
